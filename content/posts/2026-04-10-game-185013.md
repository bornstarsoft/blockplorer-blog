In a shocking revelation, employees of Gunzilla Games, the blockchain game development studio co-founded by acclaimed director Neill Blomkamp, have alleged that they have not been paid for several months. This situation raises serious concerns about the treatment of staff in the gaming industry, particularly in companies that are still finding their footing in a rapidly evolving market.

Former animator Paul Creamer took to LinkedIn to share his experience, claiming he has not received his wages since October 2025. His post has sparked a wave of similar complaints from other former employees, highlighting a troubling pattern of delayed payments and lack of communication from upper management. The allegations suggest a culture of silence and fear, where employees are discouraged from speaking out about their grievances.

As the gaming industry continues to embrace blockchain technology and NFTs, the ethical implications of how companies treat their employees cannot be overlooked. Will this situation prompt a larger conversation about labor rights in tech and gaming, or will it fade into the background as just another cautionary tale?
