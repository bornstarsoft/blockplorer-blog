In a disheartening turn of events for the gaming community, Full Circle, the studio behind the highly anticipated new iteration of Skate, has announced layoffs as part of a restructuring effort. This news comes just ahead of the game's full release, which has already generated mixed reviews during its Early Access phase. The layoffs serve as a stark reminder of the volatility within the AAA gaming industry, where even promising projects can be overshadowed by financial challenges.

Founded in 2021 as a subsidiary of Electronic Arts, Full Circle's announcement highlighted the contributions of those affected, noting their integral role in building the foundation of the Skate franchise. As the gaming landscape continues to evolve, the impact of these layoffs raises questions about the future of skate. and the direction of live-service games. With the original Skate series having set a high bar for realism and gameplay, fans are left wondering how these changes will affect their beloved franchise.

As we look ahead, the fate of skate. remains uncertain, but the community's passion for the series is undeniable. Will Full Circle be able to navigate these challenges and deliver a game that meets the expectations of long-time fans? Only time will tell.
